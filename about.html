EXO

Exo (Korean: 엑소, often written as EXO) is a South Korean-Chinese male vocal group based in Seoul. The group consists of nine members: Suho, Baekhyun, Chanyeol, D.O., Kai, Sehun, Xiumin, Lay, and Chen. The group was formed by SM Entertainment in 2011 and debuted in 2012. Their music incorporates genres such as hip hop, contemporary R&B, and also electronic dance music genres such as house, trap, and synth-pop. Exo releases and performs music in Korean, Mandarin, and Japanese. The group placed in the top five as the most influential celebrity on the Forbes Korea Power Celebrity list every year from 2014 to 2018, and has been dubbed "the world's biggest boy band" and "kings of K-pop" by the media. The group debuted with 12 members divided into two subgroups: EXO-K (Suho, Baekhyun, Chanyeol, D.O., Kai, and Sehun) and EXO-M (Xiumin, Lay, Chen, and former members such as Kris, Luhan, and Tao). EXO-K and EXO-M performed music in Korean and Chinese, respectively, until 2014. EXO has been one group since then, continuing to release and perform music in multiple languages. After the departure of Kris, Luhan, and Tao amid lawsuits in 2014 and 2015, the group has remained with nine members. Since 2016, Chen, Baekhyun, and Xiumin have also released music and performed as a sub-unit called Exo-CBX. All EXO members also have solo careers in fields such as music, film, and television.

EXO's first album XOXO (2013), which produced the breakthrough hit "Growl", was a critical and commercial success. The song sold over one million copies, making EXO the best-selling Korean artist in 12 years. Subsequent albums and EPs also received high sales, with all studio albums selling over one million copies each. Studio albums XOXO (2013), Exodus (2015) and Ex'Act (2016),' along with EXO's third EP Overdose (2014), won EXO several awards including four consecutive wins each of Disc Daesang at the Golden Disc Awards, Album of the Year at the Mnet Asian Music Awards and Daesang award at the Seoul Music Awards. Their fourth album The War (2017) became their best-selling album after its release, selling over 1.6 million copies in South Korea and winning a second consecutive Artist of the Year award at the Melon Music Awards, as well as a record fifth consecutive Album of the Year award at the Mnet Asian Music Awards. EXO's fifth album Don't Mess Up My Tempo (2018) was their highest-placed album on the US Billboard 200, debuting at number 23. It was also their best-selling album with over 1.9 million copies in South Korea.

Since EXO's 2014 headline tour, the group has performed at over 100 concerts in four tours and has participated in several joint tours. EXO is also known for its work outside of music, which includes sponsorship deals with brands such as Nature Republic and Samsung, and charitable acts such as Smile For U, a joint project of SM Entertainment and UNICEF that began in 2015, in which EXO continues to participate.

HISTORY

2006-2012: Formations and debuts

EXO-K's leader Suho was the first member to join SM Entertainment, after a successful audition through the company's member selection system in 2006. The following year, Kai, with the confidence of his father, auditioned for the SM Youth Best Contest; he won and got a contract. Chanyeol, who came second in the Smart Model Contest, and Sehun, who auditioned four times in two years, were the next members to enter training in 2008. In 2010, D.O. of Exo-K sang at his audition and was eventually offered a contract. The last member of Exo-K who went through training was Baekhyun, who joined in 2011 through SM Entertainment's selection system and trained for about a year before debuting. In Exo-M, Kris auditioned in 2008 at an SM Entertainment global audition in Canada, before moving to South Korea for training. In the same year, Lay auditioned in Changsha, China, and then moved to South Korea, and Xiumin auditioned with his friend and won second place. In 2010, Luhan was discovered by a representative from SM Entertainment in Seoul and passed his audition, and Tao was discovered at a talent show. The last member to join Exo-M was Chen, who joined the member selection system in 2011.

Speaking at a business seminar at Stanford University in May 2011, SM Entertainment founder Lee Soo-man shared a strategy to debut a new boy band that would be divided into two sub-groups, promoting the same music simultaneously in South Korea and China by performing songs in Korean and Mandarin. After several member changes, in December 2011, the group was finally named as Exo, after the word "exoplanet". They are divided into two sub-groups: Exo-K for South Korea and Exo-M for China. The group's first television appearance was on the annual SBS Gayo Daejeon show on 29 December 2011.

Exo-K and Exo-M debuted with the single "Mama" on 8 April 2012, followed by the Mama EP on 9 April. The two sub-groups promoted the album separately; Exo-K performed on South Korean music programme The Music Trend and Exo-M performed at the Top Chinese Music Awards in Shenzhen on the same day. The Korean version of the EP was number one on South Korea's Gaon Album Chart and number eight on the Billboard World Albums Chart, and the Chinese version was number two on China's Sina Album Chart and number one on various Chinese online apps. After the debut, Exo released two singles, titled "What Is Love" and "History", which came in at number 88 and number 68 respectively on the Gaon Digital Chart and at number six on the Sina Music Chart. Exo was awarded Best New Asian Group at the 2012 Mnet Asian Music Awards and the Newcomer Award at the Golden Disc Awards.

2013-2014: Commercial success, first headline tour, and Kris and Luhan lawsuits

Their first studio album, XOXO, was released on 3 June 2013, in two versions: one Korean and one in Mandarin. Unlike the Mama EP, for which Exo-K and Exo-M had separate promotions, XOXO was promoted together, mostly in South Korea. Exo recorded the lead single of the album, "Wolf" together, but the other songs of the album were recorded separately. A new version of the album, titled Growl, was released on 5 August 2013, with three additional songs. The new single titled "Growl" was at number three on the Billboard K-Pop Hot 100 and at number two on the Gaon Digital Chart. All versions of XOXO collectively sold over one million copies, making Exo the first South Korean artist to achieve this milestone in 12 years. In December that year, Exo released a second EP, Miracles in December, as a winter special album that produced a single titled the same as the album title. Following the release, the group promoted the album through their first reality show, Exo's Showtime, which premiered on 28 November 2013 on MBC's Every 1 cable channel. Following their 2013 release, Exo won Song of the Year at the Melon Music Awards for "Growl", Disc Daesang at the Golden Disc Awards and Album of the Year at the Mnet Asian Music Awards[41] for XOXO, and Daesang at the Seoul Music Awards. In addition, Exo-M received the Most Popular Group Award at the Top Chinese Music Awards 2013.

Exo's third EP, Overdose, was released on 7 May 2014. Originally scheduled for release on 21 April, the album was delayed due to the Sewol Ferry Accident on 16 April. Overdose was promoted similarly to Mama, with Exo-K in South Korea and Exo-M promoting it mostly in China. The album passed 660,000 pre-orders, making it the most pre-ordered Korean EP in history. Its Korean edition also came in at number two on the Billboard World Albums Chart, and at number 129 on the Billboard 200, making Exo the highest-placed male Korean group on these album charts at the time. Overdose became the best-selling song of 2014 in South Korea, and became the first EP to top the annual charts and win the Album of the Year Award at the Mnet Asian Music Awards. On 22 December, Exo released their first live album, Exology Chapter 1: The Lost Planet. The album single, "December, 2014 (The Winter's Tale)", peaked at number one on the Gaon Digital Chart, making it Exo's first number one single. At the end of 2014, Exo became the best-selling K-pop artist in Japan that year.

On 15 May 2014, SM Entertainment confirmed that Kris had filed a lawsuit to terminate his contract. On 24 May, the group kicked off their first headline tour, Exo from Exoplanet #1 - The Lost Planet, at the Olympic Gymnastics Arena. Tickets for this concert were sold in 1.47 seconds, breaking the record as the fastest concert sale by a Korean artist. On 10 October, Luhan also filed a lawsuit against SM Entertainment to terminate his contract.

2015: Critical acclaim, Tao lawsuit and Japanese debut

On 7 March 2015, Exo kicked off their second world tour, Exo Planet 2 - The Exo'luxion|Exoplanet 2 - The Exo'luxion, with 70,000 spectators over five nights at the Olympic Gymnastics Arena in Seoul. The group released its second studio album, Exodus on 30 March 2015, in Korean and Chinese versions. Domestic pre-orders for the album surpassed 500,000 copies in less than 24 hours, breaking a new record for pre-orders. The lead single, "Call Me Baby" was released earlier than scheduled, on 27 March, as a version of the song had leaked online. The music video was released four days later; the Korean version went on to become the most watched K-pop music video in the first six months of 2015. The album spent four consecutive weeks at number one on the Gaon Album Chart and sold over one million copies, making it Exo's second album to do so, after XOXO. Exodus won Album of the Year at the 2015 Mnet Asian Music Awards, becoming Exo's third consecutive win. In April 2015, Exodus was at number 95 on the US Billboard 200, making the group the highest-ranked Korean artist on this album chart at that time. Exo also charted on the Billboard Canadian Hot 100 at number 98, making them the first K-pop group and second Korean artist to chart on this chart.

Tao was absent during the promotion of Exodus due to injury and, on 24 August, became the third member to file a lawsuit against SM Entertainment to terminate his contract. Exo released a new version of Exodus, titled Love Me Right, on 3 June 2015. This edition added four new songs, including a single titled "Love Me Right". Due to Tao's absence, the group promoted the song with nine members. In October that year, Exo became the first artist to hold a dome concert in South Korea; it was called Exo - Love Concert in Dome, and took place at the Gocheok Sky Dome in Seoul.

On 4 November 2015, Exo released their Japanese debut single, Love Me Right ~romantic universe~, which contained the Japanese version of the title track "Love Me Right" and the original Japanese song "Drop That". On the day of release, the album sold 147,000 copies and topped the Oricon album chart, becoming the best-selling debut single of all time in Japan by a Korean artist. Five days later, Exo released a special single titled "Lightsaber" to promote the film Star Wars: The Force Awakens upon its release in South Korea, as part of a collaboration between SM Entertainment and Walt Disney. On 10 December, Exo released its second winter special edition and fourth EP, Sing for You, which produced singles such as "Sing for You" and "Unfair". The album sold 267,900 copies in its first week, breaking the record for the highest first-week sales by a Korean artist. "Unfair" also became the first K-pop song to be included on Apple Music's "Best of the Week" playlist, and Exo became the first Korean artist to be featured on the platform's homepage. A portion of the profits from the album was donated to UNICEF's Smile For U campaign, to support music education for children in Asia. In December 2015, South Korea's entertainment industry leader placed Exo at number seven in its top ten as cultural leaders of 2015, the highest ranking held by a K-pop artist.

2016-2017: Solo ventures and global success

Exo Ex'Act' s third studio album and its singles, "Lucky One" and "Monster", were released on 9 June 2016, in Korean and Chinese versions. Domestic pre-orders for the album surpassed 660,000 copies, making it the K-pop album with the most pre-orders at that time. The album broke the record for the highest first-week sales by a Korean language album, previously held by their own fourth EP, Sing For You. "Monster" became Exo's first number one song on the Billboard World Digital Songs chart, and "Lucky One" debuted at number one. On 18 August 2016, a new edition of the album, titled Lotto, was released; it added four new songs, including a single titled the same as the album title. "Lotto" became Exo's second number one song on the Billboard World Digital Songs chart, and came in at number two on the Gaon Digital Chart. The new editions of the Korean and Chinese versions were number one and two on the Gaon Album Chart, respectively. Less than two months from its official release, Ex'Act had sold 1.17 million copies, becoming Exo's third album to sell over one million copies, earning them the title of "three million seller" from the media.

On 21 July, Kris and Luhan officially left Exo as their contract dispute with SM Entertainment was resolved. On 22 July, Exo held their third headline tour, "Exoplanet 3 - The Exo'rdium", with a record six consecutive concerts at the Olympic Gymnastics Arena. On 28 May 2017, the tour ended with the second of two consecutive concerts at Seoul Olympic Stadium, the largest stadium in South Korea, with an estimated maximum capacity of 100,000. Tickets for the first date went on sale on 12 April and sold out in less than 20 minutes. In June, Lay announced that he would not take part in the group's next album, choosing instead to focus on his acting career. Since then, he has been absent from albums and public appearances with Exo. On 7 December, Exo released their second Japanese single, "Coming Over". Selling over 158,000 copies, the single peaked at number two on the Oricon weekly chart and became their second Japanese single to be certified Platinum by the Recording Industry Association of Japan. Exo's third winter release and fifth EP titled For Life was released on 19 December. They did not publicly promote the album, which sold over 442,000 copies in less than two weeks.

Also in 2016, the group members spent time developing their solo careers. On 7 January, Baekhyun released "Dream", a collaboration with South Korean singer Suzy. The single became popular, and won Best Collaboration at the 18th Mnet Asian Music Awards among other awards. On 31 October, Chen, Baekhyun, and Xiumin debuted as the first sub-unit of the group, Exo-CBX, which is a combination of the initials of each member's stage name. As of 2018, they have released two EPs in Korean and two EPs in Japanese. On 28 October, Lay released the first of his two solo EPs in Chinese. In 2016 he also acted in The Mystic Nine, a television show in China that attracted over 12 billion views online. All members of the group also pursue solo activities in various industries, including music, film, television, and theatre.

Exo 's fourth studio album The War was released on 18 July 2017. The album received 807,235 pre-orders, surpassing their own record of 660,000 for Ex'Act.[95 ] Lead single "Ko Ko Bop" debuted at number one on the Melon Digital Chart, making Exo the first K-pop group to enter number one after chart changes were implemented on 27 February 2017. Upon release, the album recorded the highest first-week sales of any K-pop album. The album debuted at number 87 on the Billboard 200, number one on the Billboard World Albums chart, and other album charts around the world-a first for Exo. On 29 August, the 2018 edition of Guinness World Records named Exo the "Most Daesang" (grand prize) award at the Mnet Asian Music Awards. On 5 September, the group released a new edition of The War, titled The War: The Power of Music. The album added three new songs, including the single "Power." On 14 September, "Power" recorded an all-time high score on M Countdown with 11,000 points, which made Exo the first artist to achieve a perfect score after changes to the show's calculation system were implemented in June 2015. This win also marked the group's 100th win on a music show."

On 19 October, Exo announced their third world tour, "Exoplanet #4 - The EℓyXiOn", which began with three consecutive nights at the Gocheok Sky Dome in Seoul, starting on 24 November. As of 30 November, Korean sales of The War reportedly reached nearly 1.6 million copies, making it Exo's best-selling album at the time and earning them the title of "four million seller" from the media. In early December, Exo announced their fourth winter special release and sixth EP, Universe. The EP was originally set to be released on 21 December, but was delayed until 26 December due to the death of fellow labelmate Jonghyun.

2018-present: Winter Olympics and international recognition

On 16 January 2018, "Power" became the first K-pop song to be played at The Dubai Fountain at Burj Khalifa Lake, Dubai. All seven members travelled to Dubai to see the first playback. The initial screening of the song was extended from January to March. The song was also played again from September to November of the same year. On 31 January, Exo released their first Japanese studio album titled Countdown. The album debuted at number one on Oricon's weekly chart, selling around 89,000 copies. This achievement made Exo the first non-Japanese music group to have both a single and a debut album at number one on the Oricon weekly chart. Ten days after its release, on 9 February, Countdown was certified Gold by the Recording Industry Association of Japan.

In early February, it was announced that Exo would be performing at the closing ceremony of the 2018 Winter Olympics in Pyeongchang on 25 February, as representatives of K-pop along with CL. Following this announcement, Baekhyun sang the national anthem at the opening ceremony of the International Olympic Committee's general assembly on 5 February, and Exo performed at the official D-100 Concert in late 2017, which marked 100 days until the start of the Winter Olympics. Their performance attracted global media attention and praise. In March 2018, the Korean Mint Corporation announced plans to create an official commemorative medal for Exo, awarded to the group for their contributions to spreading Korean culture globally. Nine medals, each commemorating one of the group's members, were revealed on 18 April, at a ceremony in Seoul.

Exo's fifth studio album, Don't Mess Up My Tempo, was announced in early October with a release date of 2 November 2018. It was also revealed Don't Mess Up My Tempo will feature all nine members of Exo- the group's first album released since Lotto in 2016. The album received 1,104,617 pre-orders, surpassing their previous record. The album gained success, selling 1,179,997 copies as of 30 November, making Exo a "five million seller" of albums in the media. With the release of Don't Mess Up My Tempo, Exo became the first artist to pass 10 million total album sales in South Korea. The album debuted at number 23 on the Billboard 200 with 23,000 album equivalent sales and number one on Billboard's Independent Albums and World Albums charts. It is Exo's highest-placed album on the Billboard 200 and their fourth number-one album on the World Albums chart. Love Shot was released on 13 December, as a new version of Don't Mess Up My Tempo. The lead single, also titled "Love Shot", became Exo's third number-one single on the Billboard World Digital Songs chart and stayed for three consecutive weeks. Don't Mess Up My Tempo sold 1,951,879 copies in South Korea in 2018 and became Exo's best-selling album.

Exo has won several awards in South Korea and internationally. On 14 June 2013, the group got their first music show win, with their single "Wolf" at Music Bank Korea. Exo holds the record for the most music video wins for a single single, with 18 wins for "Call Me Baby", released in 2015.

Awards and nominees

Exo has won several awards in South Korea and internationally. On 14 June 2013, the group got their first music show win, with their single "Wolf" at Music Bank Korea. Music programmes are weekly television shows in South Korea and China that honour artists in the country based on digital chart performance, album sales and social media popularity. Exo holds the record for the most music video wins for a single, with 18 wins for "Call Me Baby", released in 2015. Exo has won over 100 music programme awards, becoming only the second artist to do so since fellow labelmates Girls' Generation.

Exo has also received 23 Daesang awards, also known as grand prize awards, from year-end award shows. This includes six Daesang awards from the Mnet Asian Music Awards, which were entered into the 2018 edition of the book by Guinness World Records as "Most Daesang" (grand prize) awards won at the Mnet Asian Music Awards.

On 3 November 2017, Exo received a Prime Minister's Commendation at the Korean Popular Culture and Arts Awards, an award given in recognition of public service and/or excellence in a particular field. Upon receiving this award, Suho said: "We have received a great award along with brilliant people...It is a great honour. We will be entertainers who promote not only K-pop, but also Korea."
